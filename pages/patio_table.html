Patio Side Table

For this project, I designed and built a custom side table for my patio.

This piece is made from rebar and some scrap wood. Its about 20" high. We get some strong winds from time to time, so I wanted something small, but heavy so it wouldn't tip over or blow away in the wind.

I wanted to experiment with dying the wood. I used food coloring. The scrap wood was weathered so it toned down the colors, making them more earthy, which I really liked. I used a dremel with a compass attachment to scribe the ring into the wood. This prevented the two colored areas from bleeding into each other. If finished it with polyurethane. I may use dyed epoxy resin to fill the ring in. I'll post updates if I do.

To bend the hexagons and hair pin legs, I made this jig and used cast iron pipe, which I slid over the rebar, for mechanical advantage and to keep the rebar from bending in the wrong location. I may make more of these with a few design changes.
